DEFINITIONS

  1. By division of labor we understand a productive activity in which one is alienated from their creativity.

  2. That action is called monetization which produces for the purpose of exchanging its product for capital.

  3. By collective creativity we understand that process through which singular people, each according to their own capacities, come together to create something new

    Explanation. No equality exists among humans: we are not equal, we are different. Each singular human has their own different capabilities and inclinations. Whenever one pursues their inclinations and capabilities, whenever they do what they can, their activity does not consist in a division of labor; it is, rather, in the free realization of their form-of-life.

AXIOMS

  1. What is created is either created for itself, or it is produced for the purpose of something else.

  2. That which does not produce for the purpose of something else, must create for itself.

  3. From an absence of purpose, creation neccesarily follows; and, on the other hand, if a capitalist purpose is given, it is impossible that creation can follow.

  4. Creation can only happen if it is removed from monetization and the division of labor.

  5. The pursuit of singular creativity is only possible through community.

  6. Community can only exist as a decision process achieved through discussion and consensus, never debate and voting.

  7. The division of labor prevents both community and creativity

    Corollary. Hence, it follows that Orgic encourages the pursuit of one's singular capabilities, while preventing, at the same time, a division of labor. This means that while creation will often be entirely singular, editing must always be collective.

PROPOSITIONS

  1. All propositions and modes of organization and action, with the exclusion of our axioms, are subject to constant change. These are not laws, but reflections of our current general consensus.

  2. Submissions are accepted in any form of media, and there are no deadlines. Currently, the agreed upon threshold of standing submissions before the review process is ten. This review process is conducted on each of the ten pieces by all ten members of the submission group, with the exclusion of the primary creator(s) of the work currently in question. Any submission without a creator present is automatically disqualified from consideration. The quantity of submissions accepted and declined, though works which the group believes has good potential may be declined with suggestions and asked for resubmittal in the future.

  3. After the review process, those works which have been accepted form the body of drafts for the next edition. Times are scheduled for the collective editing process, where all contributors, including the creator, engage in creative critique to expand and improve the works in question until all contributors have reached a consensus that it is of the highest quality. Once this consensus is reached, all works in their finalized form are released as the nth edition.

  4. Authorship is neither encouraged nor discouraged, though the process of collective editiing naturally places considerable importance away from the original author.
